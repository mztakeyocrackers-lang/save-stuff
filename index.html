Mission Statement

The Statewide Anti-Violence Enforcement Unit exists to identify, deter, and suppress violent criminal activity throughout the State of Illinois — with particular emphasis on firearm-related offenses, repeat violent offenders, and high-risk crime corridors.

Core Values

  • Professionalism in all conduct, on and off duty
  • Accountability to the public and command
  • Strict adherence to law and policy
  • Intelligence-driven, strategic enforcement
  • Respect for individual rights and dignity

Operational Authority

SAVE derives authority from the Illinois Compiled Statutes, Illinois State Police General Orders, Patrol and Criminal Investigation Directives, and constitutional protections under both the United States and Illinois Constitutions.

Section 1 — Purpose and Authority

§ 1.01 – 1.02
1.01 — Purpose
The Statewide Anti-Violence Enforcement (SAVE) Unit exists to identify, deter, and suppress violent criminal activity throughout the State of Illinois, with particular emphasis on firearm-related offenses, repeat violent offenders, and high-risk crime corridors.

All actions undertaken by SAVE personnel shall reflect professionalism, accountability, and strict adherence to the law. SAVE operates under the authority of the Illinois State Police through proactive, intelligence-driven enforcement strategies.
1.02 — Authority
SAVE personnel derive authority from the Illinois Compiled Statutes, Illinois State Police General Orders, Patrol and Criminal Investigation Directives, and constitutional protections under both the United States and Illinois Constitutions.
No member assigned to SAVE shall exceed the lawful authority granted to sworn members of the Illinois State Police.

Section 2 — Mission and Organization

§ 2.01 – 2.03
2.01 — Mission Objectives
The mission of SAVE is to reduce violent crime through targeted enforcement operations based on intelligence and crime analysis. The unit is responsible for:
  • Interdiction of illegal firearms across all operational areas
  • Apprehension of violent and repeat offenders
  • Collaboration with local, county, and federal agencies to suppress criminal activity
  • Maintaining a visible, professional deterrent presence in high-risk areas
2.02 — Organizational Structure
SAVE operates within the established command framework of the Illinois State Police. Personnel are selected based on performance, experience, and demonstrated professionalism. Assignments may include uniformed or plainclothes operations depending on operational requirements.
2.03 — Command Oversight
Operational oversight is assigned to designated Patrol or Investigative Command staff. Daily supervision is maintained by a SAVE Supervisor holding the rank of Sergeant or above. Command staff retain full discretion over deployment decisions, assignment status, and operational priorities.

Section 3 — Deployment and Operations

§ 3.01 – 3.03
3.01 — Intelligence-Driven Deployment
SAVE deployments shall be based on credible intelligence, crime trend analysis, repeat offender tracking, and interagency information sharing. Deployment decisions must be strategic and documented. Arbitrary or unsupported deployments are not authorized.
3.02 — Operational Briefings
Prior to every enforcement operation, supervisory personnel shall conduct a structured briefing covering:
  • Target locations and threat assessments
  • Officer safety considerations
  • Body-worn camera compliance requirements ( Medal/A form of Clipping-Software is Required prior to any patrol, unless on a device that prohibits it. )
  • Arrest procedures and use-of-force reminders
  • Inter-unit coordination and communication assignments
3.03 — Enforcement Operations
Authorized operations include saturation patrols, directed patrols in designated areas, fugitive apprehension assistance, firearm interdiction efforts, and coordinated multi-agency enforcement details.
All operations must remain lawful, professional, and clearly documented. Operational improvisation that falls outside this SOP requires prior supervisory approval.

Section 4 — Enforcement Standards

§ 4.01 – 4.04
4.01 — Lawful Stops and Detentions
All stops, detentions, and searches conducted by SAVE personnel must be supported by reasonable suspicion or probable cause consistent with constitutional standards. Members shall articulate the legal basis for all enforcement actions within official reports. Stops lacking documented justification are not permitted.
4.02 — Firearm Enforcement Procedures
Firearm enforcement actions must comply with state statute and agency policy. Upon recovery of any firearm:
  • Secure the firearm safely and immediately
  • Process as evidence in accordance with chain-of-custody procedures
  • Document the recovery fully — location, circumstances, and involved individuals
  • Submit required reports prior to end of shift
4.03 — Documentation Requirements
All enforcement activity must be documented accurately and completely prior to the end of shift unless otherwise authorized by a supervisor. Reports must reflect objective facts, lawful justification, and a complete account of the enforcement action taken.
4.04 — Utilization of SAVE Equipment
SAVE Department equipment — including department vehicles, issued uniforms, and any other SAVE-designated resources — are to be used exclusively during active SAVE deployments. Members are expected to treat all issued equipment with care and professionalism.

Unless explicitly authorized by SAVE Command, members are not permitted to utilize any SAVE equipment outside of an active deployment. Members found in violation will receive a verbal or written warning; continued disregard may result in further disciplinary action as determined by Command Staff.

Section 5 — Use of Force

§ 5.01 – 5.02
5.01 — Policy Compliance
SAVE personnel are bound by Illinois State Police Use of Force policy at all times. Force must be:
  • Objectively reasonable given the circumstances
  • Proportional to the threat presented
  • Consistent with constitutional standards and agency policy
Any use of force that does not meet these three standards is a policy violation and will be treated as a serious infraction under Section 9 of this SOP.
5.02 — Reporting and Review
Any reportable use of force requires immediate supervisory notification and completion of all required documentation. All incidents shall undergo administrative review to ensure compliance with policy and law. Members involved in a use-of-force incident shall not delay reporting.

Section 6 — Arrest and Processing

§ 6.01 – 6.02
6.01 — Custodial Arrest Procedures
Members shall confirm probable cause prior to placing any individual under arrest. Upon arrest, the following procedures apply:
  • Conduct a lawful search incident to arrest
  • Ensure safe custody procedures are followed throughout transport
  • Transport individuals in accordance with agency standards
  • Complete all required processing documentation prior to end of shift
6.02 — Multi-Agency Arrest Protocol
During joint operations, the primary arresting authority must be clearly identified and documented before the operation begins. Coordination with prosecutorial entities shall occur when charges are being considered. Ambiguity in arresting authority is not acceptable.

Section 7 — Interagency Coordination

§ 7.01 – 7.02
7.01 — Notification and Jurisdiction
When operationally appropriate, SAVE shall notify the local jurisdiction prior to conducting large-scale enforcement operations within their area. SAVE maintains statewide authority but shall respect jurisdictional relationships and coordinate proactively where possible.
7.02 — Unified Command Structure
All joint operations require a clearly defined command structure established before execution. Responsibilities — including operational command, communications lead, and documentation authority — shall be assigned and confirmed during the pre-operation briefing.

Section 8 — Professional Conduct

§ 8.01 – 8.02
8.01 — Ethical Standards
All SAVE personnel are expected to conduct themselves with the highest level of professionalism at all times — on duty, off duty, and in all communications. This includes:
  • Treating all individuals with dignity and respect regardless of circumstances
  • Exercising impartial judgment free from personal bias
  • Refusing to participate in or tolerate misconduct by any member
  • Reporting observed violations through the appropriate chain of command
8.02 — Integrity and Public Trust
The effectiveness of SAVE depends on public confidence in the unit's integrity. Members shall not engage in conduct — on or off duty — that would bring discredit to SAVE or the Illinois State Police. Any member whose conduct compromises public trust is subject to removal from the unit.

Section 9 — Disciplinary Code

§ 9.01 – 9.06
9.01 — General Provisions
Violations of this SOP or Illinois State Police directives may result in disciplinary action. Discipline may be progressive where appropriate; however, serious violations may warrant immediate and severe action regardless of prior record.
9.02 – 9.04 — Infraction Tiers
Infractions are classified into three tiers:
TierExamplesSanctions
MINORAdministrative deficiencies, minor equipment violations, incomplete documentation without malicious intentCorrective counseling, written reprimand, performance review
MODERATENegligent conduct, failure to activate recording equipment, unprofessional behavior, improper evidence handlingFormal reprimand, suspension, retraining, removal from SAVE assignment
SERIOUSAbuse of authority, unlawful detention, excessive force, falsification of records, criminal conductImmediate removal from SAVE, administrative leave, termination, criminal referral
9.05 — Investigative Review Classifications
  • Sustained — the allegation is supported by evidence
  • Not Sustained — insufficient evidence to confirm or refute the allegation
  • Unfounded — the alleged conduct did not occur
  • Exonerated — the conduct occurred but was lawful and within policy
9.06 — Removal from SAVE
SAVE is a selective assignment, not a guaranteed position. Command staff may remove a member from the unit at any time for performance deficiencies, conduct concerns, operational necessity, or loss of confidence — with or without a formal disciplinary finding.
Removal from SAVE does not automatically constitute separation from the agency. However, removal may be accompanied by additional disciplinary action depending on the circumstances.

Section 10 — Operational Security and Reporting

§ 10.01 – 10.02
10.01 — Operational Security
Operational details, target locations, and enforcement strategies shall remain confidential prior to and during execution. Members shall not discuss operational specifics outside of authorized channels.
  • Media inquiries shall be referred to the Illinois State Police Public Information Office
  • Tactical details shall not be transmitted over unsecured channels
  • Unauthorized disclosure of operational information is a serious infraction under Section 9
10.02 — Reporting and Performance Review
SAVE operations shall track the following enforcement metrics:
  • Firearms recovered
  • Arrests made and charges filed
  • Warrants served
  • Measurable impact on violent crime trends in target areas
Multi-day operations require formal after-action reporting and supervisory review prior to closure.

Section 11 — Radio Usage

§ 11.01 – 11.03
11.01 — Voice Communication Requirement (VC)
All SAVE personnel are required to maintain active voice communication capability while engaged in any operational deployment. Members shall remain connected to the designated operational channel for the duration of their assignment unless directed otherwise by a supervisor.
Text-based communication alone is not authorized during active enforcement operations. Failure to maintain required voice comms may result in administrative review and removal from the detail.
11.02 — Radio Callout Procedures
During all enforcement operations, SAVE personnel shall use the standardized callouts below:

11.03 — Professional Radio Conduct
The following are prohibited during active operations:
  • Non-essential or personal conversation over the operational channel
  • Transmission of sensitive tactical details over unsecured channels (except for officer safety)
  • Unauthorized radio silence during enforcement operations
  • Failure to use required callout formats

Section 12 — Uniform & Appearance Standards

§ 12.01 – 12.03
12.01 — Standard Uniform Requirements
All SAVE personnel operating in a uniformed capacity must wear the officially issued SAVE uniform in full and without unauthorized modification. The following standards apply at all times during uniformed deployment:
  • The issued SAVE uniform must be worn exactly as assigned — no substitutions, mix-and-match, or modifications are permitted without command approval
  • SAVE identification (badge, plate, or digital equivalent) must be visible and accessible at all times during active duty
  • Uniform appearance must be clean, professional, and presentable — worn in a manner that reflects the standards of the Illinois State Police
  • Personal cosmetic items or accessories not included in the issued loadout are not authorized for uniformed deployment
Uniform violations discovered during a deployment must be corrected immediately. Repeated uniform non-compliance is subject to disciplinary action under Section 9 of this SOP.
12.02 — Plainclothes and Undercover Operations
When authorized by SAVE Command for plainclothes or covert operations, members are not required to wear the standard uniform. However, the following requirements remain in effect:
  • Members must maintain a professional appearance appropriate to the operational context
  • SAVE credentials must be available for immediate presentation if challenged or if an arrest is made
  • Plainclothes authority must be granted in advance by a SAVE Supervisor or higher — self-authorization is not permitted
  • Members shall not represent themselves as civilian personnel while conducting enforcement actions
12.03 — Off-Duty Appearance and SAVE Branding
SAVE uniforms, markings, and insignia are official law enforcement identifiers and may not be worn or displayed in an off-duty context without explicit authorization from Command. Misuse of SAVE branding — including unauthorized use in other servers, communities, or personal promotional material — is a violation of this SOP and may constitute impersonation under the Rules & Regulations.

Section 13 — Vehicle Operations

§ 13.01 – 13.04
13.01 — Authorized Vehicle Use
SAVE-issued vehicles are department property and may only be operated during active, authorized deployments. Members are expected to operate all vehicles in a safe, legal, and professional manner consistent with in-game traffic law and department policy. The following rules apply:
  • Only personnel actively assigned to a deployment may operate a SAVE vehicle
  • SAVE vehicles may not be used for personal transportation, non-duty activities, or to assist non-SAVE individuals during deployment
  • Any vehicle damage sustained during a deployment must be reported to the on-duty supervisor before end of shift
  • Reckless operation of a SAVE vehicle — including unnecessary high-speed driving, collisions, or unsafe maneuvers outside of authorized pursuits — is a disciplinary offense
13.02 — Emergency Vehicle Operation
Emergency lights and sirens may only be activated under the following circumstances:
  • During an active traffic stop, felony stop, or Code 5
  • During an active or authorized vehicle pursuit (10-80)
  • When responding to a priority call as directed by a supervisor
  • When providing emergency escort under supervisory authorization
Activation of emergency equipment outside of these situations — including for roleplay, entertainment, or to clear traffic without cause — is a policy violation subject to disciplinary action.
13.03 — Vehicle Pursuit Policy
Vehicle pursuits (10-80) carry a high risk of injury and collateral consequences and must be managed carefully. The following standards govern all pursuits:
  • A pursuit may only be initiated when a subject has fled following a lawful stop attempt and there is reasonable basis to believe continued pursuit is warranted
  • The initiating unit must immediately call out the pursuit using the 10-80 format defined in Section 11.02 and provide location updates every 10–15 seconds
  • A supervisor must be notified of any active pursuit as soon as feasible
  • A supervisor or on-scene senior officer may terminate a pursuit at any time if the risk to public safety outweighs the benefit of apprehension — this order must be followed immediately
  • Ramming, boxing, or using physical contact to disable a fleeing vehicle requires supervisory authorization unless an immediate threat to life exists
Unauthorized termination of a pursuit order, or continuation of a pursuit after a supervisor has called it off, is a serious infraction under Section 9 of this SOP.
13.04 — Vehicle Damage and Reporting
All vehicle damage incurred during a deployment — regardless of cause — must be reported to the on-duty supervisor before the member goes off duty. The report must include the location and circumstances of the damage. Failure to report vehicle damage is treated as a negligence violation under Section 9.

Section 14 — Leave of Absence & Inactivity

§ 14.01 – 14.04
14.01 — Leave of Absence (LOA) Requests
Any SAVE personnel who anticipate being unable to participate for a period of five (5) days or longer must submit a Leave of Absence request to their immediate supervisor or SAVE Command. LOA requests must include:
  • The expected start and end date of the leave
  • A brief reason for the absence (personal reasons is acceptable — detailed disclosure is not required)
  • Confirmation that the member intends to return to active duty
LOA requests submitted with proper notice and good faith will generally be approved. Members on an approved LOA are considered in good standing and retain their rank and position during the leave period.
14.02 — Unapproved Inactivity
Personnel who are absent for seven (7) or more consecutive days without submitting a LOA request or notifying Command shall be considered Inactive Without Notice. The following progressive actions may apply:
  • 7–13 days: The member's status will be updated to Inactive in the Personnel Tracker. A welfare check message will be sent via Discord.
  • 14–20 days: The member will be placed on administrative hold and may be removed from active assignments pending contact.
  • 21+ days: The member may be removed from SAVE entirely at Command's discretion. Position may be backfilled.
Exceptional circumstances — including real-life emergencies — may be taken into account. Members who return following extended inactivity and provide reasonable context may be considered for reinstatement at Command's discretion.
14.03 — Activity Expectations
All active SAVE personnel are expected to participate in at least one (1) deployment or sanctioned SAVE activity per week during periods when they are not on an approved LOA. Consistent failure to meet this minimum activity standard — without an approved LOA — may be treated as passive inactivity and subject to review. Command reserves the right to adjust activity requirements based on operational pace and server activity levels.
14.04 — Return from Leave
Members returning from an approved LOA must notify their supervisor prior to resuming active duties. Upon return, the member's status in the Personnel Tracker will be updated to Active. Members returning from extended inactivity (21+ days) may be required to complete a brief operational reintegration confirmation with a supervisor before resuming full duties.

Section 3 — Natural Rights

Guaranteed to all personnel — non-negotiable

Natural Rights are guaranteed to every user participating in any unit within SAVE. These rights may not be suspended, denied, or circumvented under any circumstances by any individual or unit — regardless of rank or role.

3.1 — Freedom of Speech and Opinion

All users hold the right to free speech and expression of opinion. This right does not extend to speech that incites violence, promotes unlawful action, or targets individuals with the intent to harm.

3.2 — Innocent Until Proven Guilty

No user may be treated as guilty prior to a finding supported by proper, verified evidence. Accusations alone do not constitute grounds for punishment.

3.3 — Bias Prohibition

Users may not be excluded, targeted, or treated unfairly based on personal beliefs, religion, race, gender identity, or physical and mental disability. Equal treatment is non-negotiable.

3.4 — Freedom of Choice

No SAVE personnel may be held against their will for choosing to leave or join the department. However, any member who departs without reasonable cause or notice will not be considered for re-entry into the SAVE Department.

Data Collection Notice

This site may collect or process account identifiers, email addresses, role assignments, personnel roster entries, Roblox and Discord identifiers, strike records, timestamps, login-attempt metadata, local browser storage entries, and security telemetry generated by user interactions with protected features.
